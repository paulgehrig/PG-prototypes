Scenario 2: Education + Community Service

Alex applies for Medicaid and attests to half-time student enrollment. State data shows she's enrolled less than half-time. Emmy helps Alex document her credit hours, identify the gap, add community service hours, and submit a complete report.

The process

This demo walks through how state systems and Emmy work together when education alone doesn't meet CE requirements.

What is Emmy?

Emmy (Eligibility Made Easy) is a secure online tool that helps you provide additional information to complete your Medicaid application. It's designed to make the verification process simple and convenient.

What you need to do

1

Upload documentation for your student enrollment

You reported being enrolled as a half-time student at State University. The National Student Clearinghouse shows you're enrolled less than half-time. We need documentation to verify your enrollment status.

Acceptable documents include your current class schedule, enrollment verification letter, or transcript showing credit hours.

2

Add additional activities, if needed

You may also add information about other activities that count toward your eligibility, such as volunteer work, community service, internships, or part-time work.

3

Review and submit

Review your information, agree to the terms, and submit. Your eligibility worker will review your documents and make a determination.
